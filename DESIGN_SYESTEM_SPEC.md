## Clearline7 Brand System Spec (v0)

### 1. Brand Essence

- **Positioning:** Clearline7 is the confident expert that gives teams one clear, governed way to write and format professional communication.  
- **Role:** Top-level standard for how things are written and laid out across products, projects, and partners.  
- **Core Promise:** Every Clearline7 document reads and looks like it comes from the same competent mind.

***

### 2. Voice and Tone

**Voice Archetype:** Confident Expert  
- Clear, direct, and concise.  
- Helpful and instructive, never chatty or cute.  
- Low-jargon; explains when needed, doesn’t impress for its own sake.

**Tone Guidelines:**
- Default: Calm, assured, neutral-positive.  
- For rules/governance: Firmer, unambiguous, no hedging language (“must,” “required,” “not permitted”).  
- For onboarding/help: Encouraging, practical, step-by-step (“Here’s how to…”).

**Do:**
- “Use this format to ensure every memo is easy to review and archive.”  
- “If you’re writing for external partners, choose the Federal Flow style set.”

**Don’t:**
- “We kind of prefer if you would maybe format…”  
- Jokes, sarcasm, or slang in system copy.

***

### 3. Logo and Usage

**Primary Mark:**  
- Wordmark “Clearline7” in a modern, geometric sans-serif (e.g., Inter/Satoshi equivalent).  
- “Clearline” and “7” visually distinguished (weight or color shift).

**Construction:**
- Horizontal lockup only (no stacked variations in v0).  
- Clear space: at least 1× cap height around all sides.  
- Minimum size: small-screen width where “Clearline7” remains legible (define px later in UI spec).

**Do:**
- Use on white or very light backgrounds.  
- Pair with primary deep blue as default.

**Don’t:**
- Add outlines, shadows, gradients, or effects.  
- Place over busy backgrounds that reduce legibility.

***

### 4. Color System (v0)

- **Primary:** Deep Blue `#1E3A8A` — Headers, logo, primary actions.  
- **Secondary:** Cool Gray `#6B7280` — Body text, UI chrome, dividers.  
- **Accent:** Soft Teal `#14B8A6` — Links, highlights, success / “governed flexibility” states.  
- **Backgrounds:**  
  - Base: White `#FFFFFF`  
  - Alt: Off-White `#F9FAFB` for panels/sections.

**Usage Rules:**
- Deep Blue is the anchor: navigation, key headings, logo.  
- Teal is sparing: calls-to-action, tags, key callouts.  
- Gray handles structure: captions, secondary text, borders.

***

### 5. Typography

**Primary Typeface:** Modern sans-serif (system: Inter or equivalent).  

- Headings (H1–H3):  
  - Weight: SemiBold/Bold  
  - Letterspacing: Slightly open  
  - Tone: Clear hierarchy, no decorative styles.  

- Body:  
  - Weight: Regular  
  - Line height: 1.5–1.7 for readability.  

- Code / technical snippets (if needed):  
  - Monospace (e.g., JetBrains Mono) with consistent sizing.

**Rules:**
- One primary family across product, docs, and marketing.  
- Avoid mixing more than one sans + one mono.

***

### 6. Application Rules

**Structural / Governance Content (rules, standards, compliance):**
- Voice: firm, precise.  
- Visual: Deep Blue + Gray; no or minimal teal.  

**Onboarding / Help / Guides:**
- Voice: supportive, directive.  
- Visual: Introduce teal for callouts, diagrams, buttons.

**Marketing / Overview:**
- Voice: confident, benefits-led, still plainspoken.  
- Visual: More white space, deep blue anchors, teal in diagrams and key lines.
